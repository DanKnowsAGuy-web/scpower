South Carolina has three energy stories. The problem runs through all of them.

Wherever your constituents live and work, they are paying more than they need to. Here is what that looks like across the state.

The Upstate

Greenville · Spartanburg · Anderson

The Upstate runs 24 hours a day. BMW, Michelin, and 300+ automotive suppliers carry continuous industrial loads, where energy cost is a direct line item in every margin calculation.

$14.8B BMW investment in Spartanburg alone

The Midlands

Columbia · Lexington · Sumter

The Midlands is the seat of state government, the military, and public education. Fort Jackson, the University of South Carolina, and state agencies run enormous facilities on taxpayer-funded budgets. Every wasted kilowatt-hour comes out of public money.

3.7M square feet at Fort Jackson alone

The Lowcountry

Charleston · Myrtle Beach · Hilton Head

The coastal economy runs on hospitality. Hotels, resorts, and restaurants operate on thin margins in a climate where the air conditioning never stops. Energy is among the top three operating costs, and the most controllable one.

$28B SC tourism running HVAC 365 days a year
